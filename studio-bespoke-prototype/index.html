Studio Bespoke / Mira, Dubai

A home learns the rhythm
of its people.

A full renovation organized around baking, gathering, and keeping the heart of the home open.

The Inherited Plan

Closed rooms. Borrowed habits.

Before the intervention, the ground floor of this Mira villa resisted the way this family actually lived. The kitchen was entirely isolated, casting shadows across the dining area and separating the cook from the family's daily movements.

The Decisive Cut

Internal walls removed.

By dismantling the central partition walls, the kitchen, dining, and living rooms were integrated into a single continuous space, allowing light to wash from front to back.

The Heart Open

The island as social anchor.

The Calacatta marble island was extended and placed centrally, serving as both a morning baking surface and a social gathering point during meals.
