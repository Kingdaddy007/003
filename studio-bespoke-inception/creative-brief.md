# Studio Bespoke Design — Creative Brief

**Status:** Working brief for concept exploration  
**Derived from:** `evidence-dossier.md`  
**Decision class:** Type 1.5 — positioning and experience architecture are costly to reverse once production begins.

## Controlling problem

Studio Bespoke has strong evidence of renovation intelligence but makes visitors work to find it. The website must convert a visitor from “this is a tasteful decorator” to “this studio can materially improve how my home works and feels, and can be trusted with the complexity of doing so.”

## Desired perception

**Studio Bespoke is a Dubai renovation studio that designs the architecture of everyday rituals: homes where light, circulation, storage, materials and bespoke detail are resolved around the way a household actually lives.**

This is a positioning target, not final homepage copy.

## Visitor response contract

| Moment | Visitor response |
| --- | --- |
| Feel | Calm confidence, then recognition: “this could make my own home work better.” |
| Understand | The studio does more than decorate: it reorganises space, use and material systems. |
| Believe | Brittany has aesthetic judgment and technical patience for a serious renovation. |
| Do | Initiate an early, qualified conversation about a substantial residential project. |

## First-known priority

The visitor must first understand that Studio Bespoke changes the practical and emotional choreography of a home.

**First-known priority:** `A home can be re-made around the life that happens inside it.`

The opening must be followed immediately by recognisable project proof, not just an atmospheric room photograph.

## Audience assumption

### Primary

Affluent Dubai homeowners planning a substantial villa, townhouse, apartment or whole-home renovation. They value visual distinction but are anxious about disruption, decision overload, poor layouts, inconsistent execution and a home that looks polished but does not suit family life.

### Secondary

Homeowners with one consequential spatial problem—a kitchen, guest suite, living/dining area, storage, bathroom or difficult room—who value technical design direction and bespoke execution.

### Gentle wrong-fit filter

- One-to-two-month urgent completion requests.
- Product-shopping or décor-only enquiries with no meaningful design problem.
- Visitors seeking generic trend replication or free design direction.

## Proof burden

1. **Flagship transformation:** Mira must show the constraint, decisive intervention and lived result as one project—not duplicate cards.
2. **Decision captions:** walls removed/open kitchen; storage and appliance integration; powder-room relocation/coffee nook; spatial role of arches.
3. **Independent authority:** Marie Claire should be editorial confirmation, not logo clutter.
4. **Material proof:** stone, timber, microcement, joinery and hardware must be connected to a job.
5. **Client trust:** use a short attributed statement about interpretation, responsibility and collaboration.
6. **Fit/process:** explain three stages and selected capacity only after desire and proof are established.

## Taste constraints

### World to evoke

Warm architectural domesticity: a calm, lived-in residence shaped by pale stone or plaster, warm timber, joined planes, framed light and considered storage.

### Image rules

- Real project photography only for spatial claims.
- Lead with wide, deep images that make layout legible.
- Show threshold, circulation, joinery, material detail and human-use cue across the sequence.
- Do not crop out the architecture that explains a claim.
- Keep Mira as one coherent image family rather than treating each room as a new brand world.

### Interface and typography posture

- Interface withdraws behind the work; navigation is clear but quiet.
- Typography has two roles: commanding spatial statements and exact proof annotations.
- Negative space must frame a decision, pause after evidence or prepare inquiry.
- Reject low-contrast text, decorative counters, generic grids and excessive all-caps tracking.

### Motion posture

Stillness is default. Motion is justified only when it reveals transformation, shifts attention from spatial problem to solution, or exposes a material/architectural threshold. Universal fade-ups and video wallpaper are rejected.

## Anti-goals

- Do not build `hero → services → project grid → testimonials → contact`.
- Do not let “bespoke,” “timeless,” “elevated,” or “luxury” carry the argument.
- Do not make founder biography the opening proof.
- Do not create a cream-and-serif template that another studio could wear.
- Do not promise worldwide delivery, budget certainty or time savings beyond verified operating proof.
- Do not use effects or generated imagery to conceal weak portfolio structure.

## Candidate narrative forms to test

| Form | Why it may fit | Risk to test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Project-led editorial | Mira has a real transformation, independent press and specific decisions | Could over-narrow the studio to one pale, calm project |
| Ritual/threshold-led | Turns the brand position into a spatial sequence | Could become poetic without enough practical proof |
| Proof-first renovation intelligence | Establishes authority before atmosphere | Could feel clinical and lose desire |

## Territory-selection criteria

A concept may advance only if it:

1. Makes renovation intelligence clear in the first meaningful sequence.
2. Creates desire without generic luxury signals.
3. Gives Mira enough room to prove a whole-home intervention.
4. Incorporates Zulal, Desert Leaf and selected commercial work without flattening them into a grid.
5. Works with still photography and has a credible mobile version.
6. Supports a selective, high-trust inquiry.
7. Can use real, licensable assets and be maintained without unnecessary CMS complexity.

## Director gate

**Verdict:** Pass as a working creative brief. It aligns evidence, perception gap, priority, proof burden, constraints and anti-goals without choosing a hero layout, palette, type system or motion treatment.
