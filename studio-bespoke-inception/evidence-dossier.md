## Facts and evidence-backed observations

### Business and founder authority

- **Fact:** Studio Bespoke is a Dubai boutique studio focused in its current About page on high-end residential renovations and curated living spaces.
- **Reported:** Brittany Guimaraes was raised in Johannesburg, has lived in Dubai for 10+ years, completed a five-year accredited Interior Design degree, and worked in international and boutique studios.
- **Reported:** The founder holds LEED GA certification; it has not been independently verified here.
- **Fact:** The portfolio includes residential renovation, small-space transformation, styling, hospitality/food-and-beverage, retail/beauty, innovation hub, and other commercial work.

### Demonstrated design behaviour

- **Fact / independent proof:** Mira was transformed by removing internal walls and reorganising the ground floor around the kitchen. Marie Claire identifies this as the decision that changed light, movement and family life.
- **Inference, high confidence:** The studio’s real distinction is not surface styling. It translates routines into architectural and joinery decisions: circulation, storage, light, adjacency and daily use.
- **Fact:** Mira is explicitly driven by an avid baker’s daily rituals. The project is about how the client lives, not only visual mood.
- **Fact:** Zulal demonstrates a styling/joinery update that works with inherited grey slate tiles; its bathroom applies microcement and brushed brass.
- **Fact:** Desert Leaf converts a former maid’s room into a self-contained guest studio through joinery, light, neutral tones and spatial efficiency.
- **Reported:** The New Earth Café testimony credits Brittany with interpreting the client vision and sourcing terracotta and clay tiles for a conscious-lifestyle brief.

### Commercial and inquiry posture

- **Fact:** The contact page says the studio curates a limited number of projects each year and may not suit one-to-two-month urgent completion requests.
- **Reported:** Turnkey work is concept development, detailed design and construction under one project-management relationship.
- **Reported:** Worldwide projects are offered. Current markets, local execution partners and operating capacity remain unproven.

## Current website and perception audit

### Perception gap

> Studio Bespoke should be perceived as a high-trust renovation partner that reshapes a home around the rituals and practical patterns of the people who inhabit it. Today it reads first as an attractive warm-minimal decorator portfolio because its strongest evidence—spatial reconfiguration, circulation, joinery, materials and client use—is buried beneath generic welcome copy, unlabelled imagery and fragmented project cards.

### Premium signals

- A real, independently published renovation with specific spatial decisions.
- Walls removed, circulation reorganised, storage integrated, a powder room relocated and a coffee nook concealed.
- Material choices connected to use: stone, timber, microcement and joinery.
- A calm fit filter for suitable project timelines.
- Founder credentials and a stated delivery network.

## Taste world and counter-standard

### Observed taste world

**Warm architectural domesticity:** calm, lived-in residences shaped by framed light, pale stone or plaster, warm timber, joined planes, concealed function, bespoke joinery and controlled material contrast. This is not generic cream-and-serif “quiet luxury”; it is spatial order, material restraint and lived ritual.

### What does not belong

- Empty beige minimalism, stock-like room imagery or generic luxury vocabulary.
- “Bespoke,” “timeless,” “elevated,” or “luxury” without a demonstrated decision.
- A portfolio dump that treats a children’s bathroom, café and major villa as equal cards.
- Generic service-card design, loud lead funnels or motion used as decoration.

### Counter-standard

> The old world says a premium home is demonstrated by a beautiful finished room. Studio Bespoke should demonstrate that a lasting home begins with changing how it is used: how light arrives, how people move, what is kept visible, what is stored away, and where daily rituals happen.

### Second-order implications

- A project-led website needs before imagery, drawings, plans, material boards, site photographs or founder commentary. Without them, proof must stay editorial and still-led.
- A renovation-first position intentionally filters rush, small and styling-only enquiries.
- A live portfolio redesign must consolidate split project entries into parent narratives.
