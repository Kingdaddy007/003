# Master Visual Storyboard: Studio Bespoke Design

This document translates our strategy into a clear, section-by-section visual map of the website. 

**The Core Mantra driving every decision:** 
> *"A great home is built around your family's daily routine... we don't just decorate, we change how you live by changing how your home is built around you."*

Because of this mantra, this website is **not** a standard portfolio grid. It is a spatial journey that proves you understand how families actually live.

Here is exactly what the visitor will experience, bit by bit, as they scroll down the page.

---

## 1. The Arrival (The Hero Section)

**The Goal:** Make the visitor feel that design begins with their life, not just pretty objects.

* **What you see:** The screen starts slightly dark or obstructed (like standing in a hallway). As the site finishes loading, the "lights turn on" or a virtual curtain pulls back to reveal a stunning, wide, deep photograph of the Mira project.
* **The Typography:** Instead of a massive, loud logo, the words **"A home learns you"** (or similar) fade quietly into the negative space (like a blank wall) of the photograph.
* **How the mantra drives this:** We aren't shouting "Luxury Interior Design Dubai." We are whispering a truth about home life, setting a calm, confident tone immediately.

---

## 2. The Inherited Plan (The Tension)

**The Goal:** Prove that you understand their frustrations before you show them the solution.

* **What you see as you scroll down:** The bright, wide photography shifts to a slightly darker, more constrained section. We show a restrained image—perhaps a tight architectural crop. 
* **The Typography:** A short, declarative statement about how existing homes (or builder-grade layouts) often resist the way modern families actually live.
* **How the mantra drives this:** By naming the problem (e.g., "Homes that force you to adapt to them"), we build trust. We show we aren't just decorators; we solve structural friction.

---

## 3. The Decisive Cut (The Flagship Proof)

**The Goal:** Prove the transformation.

* **What you see:** This is the magic moment. The screen "pins" (locks in place) for a moment as you scroll. A wide shot of the Mira project is shown. As you scroll, we might scrub through a subtle video or reveal architectural annotations pointing to exactly what you changed (e.g., "Walls removed to connect kitchen and living").
* **The Typography:** We include a subtle link/quote to the Marie Claire press feature here as independent proof.
* **How the mantra drives this:** We don't just show a pretty kitchen. We explicitly label *why* the kitchen was opened up—so the family can gather. We prove the mantra with physical architectural changes.

---

## 4. Rituals Made Spatial (The Details)

**The Goal:** Show that the big structural changes translate into small, perfect daily moments.

* **What you see:** A slow, beautiful gallery procession. We show close-up details: integrated storage, a concealed coffee nook, beautiful joinery, the curve of an arch.
* **The Typography:** Small, blueprint-like captions attached to the edges of the images. They don't say "Beautiful cabinets." They say "Concealed coffee nook to clear morning friction."
* **How the mantra drives this:** This section connects the physical materials (wood, stone, microcement) directly to the daily routines of the family.

---

## 5. The Method Travels (Scale)

**The Goal:** Prove that you can do this for any size project.

* **What you see:** A clean, split-screen or side-by-side layout showing concise snippets of two other projects (Zulal and Desert Leaf).
* **The Typography:** Editorial descriptions explaining how the same intelligence applied to the Mira villa also applies to a guest suite or a single room.
* **How the mantra drives this:** It reassures the visitor that your philosophy works regardless of the specific house or layout.

---

## 6. The Hand Behind Ease (Process & Trust)

**The Goal:** Prove you can manage the stress of a renovation.

* **What you see:** Images of the process—material sample boards, a sketch, or a professional portrait of Brittany working. 
* **The Typography:** A simple three-stage breakdown of how you work, moving from concept to completion. 
* **How the mantra drives this:** True luxury is peace of mind. We show that the process of getting that "perfect home" is handled by a professional.

---

## 7. The Invitation (Inquiry)

**The Goal:** Filter out bad clients and invite the right ones.

* **What you see:** A very quiet, calm visual field (perhaps just the warm mineral paper background color). No chaotic forms.
* **The Typography:** Instead of "Contact Us," the prompt is: *"Tell us what no longer works in the way you live at home."*
* **How the mantra drives this:** We end exactly how we started—focusing on their daily life and rituals. It forces the prospective client to think about their home's function, instantly qualifying them as a serious client rather than a window shopper.
