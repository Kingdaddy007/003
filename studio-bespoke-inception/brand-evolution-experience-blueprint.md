# Studio Bespoke — Brand-Evolution Experience Blueprint

**Status:** Review draft v1 — strategic structure only
**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Scope:** Homepage direction before concept selection or further implementation
**Code authority:** None. This document does not authorize homepage code changes, deployment, publication, form submission, commits, or pushes.

## 1. Why this blueprint exists

The previous homepage direction made Mira's renovation story the organizing argument for the entire Studio Bespoke homepage. That is a defensible project case-study direction, but it over-narrows the brand.

This blueprint restores the larger job of a studio homepage:

1. Establish the Studio Bespoke world.
2. Introduce the founder-led practice.
3. Explain the studio's services and responsibility.
4. Demonstrate range through selected work.
5. Give one project enough depth to prove judgment.
6. Build trust through approach, recognition, and client evidence.
7. Create a calm path to inquiry.

The redesign principle is **evolution, not replacement**: preserve the category, tone, content priorities, and visual preferences Studio Bespoke already demonstrates, then improve hierarchy, authorship, cinematic quality, proof, accessibility, and execution.

## 4. Revised brand diagnosis

### Perception gap

Studio Bespoke wants to present a personal, high-quality, founder-led interior design practice with substantial residential capability. The existing website contains the necessary ingredients, but its generic section treatment, long copy blocks, weak project hierarchy, inconsistent content polish, and limited spatial choreography understate the quality and range of the work.

### Desired visitor belief

> Studio Bespoke combines a personal understanding of how clients live with the design judgment and delivery responsibility required to shape a complete home.

This is a strategic belief, not proposed homepage copy.

### Taste world

**Warm architectural domesticity:** composed rooms, restrained materials, warm timber, stone or plaster tones, framed light, integrated detailing, calm object placement, and evidence of real life without visual clutter.

### Enemy and clichés to reject

- Generic beige “quiet luxury” without project specificity.
- Portfolio grids where every project has equal weight.
- Empty claims such as “timeless,” “elevated,” or “bespoke” without evidence.
- Award-site effects that overpower the interiors.
- Video wallpaper with unrelated marketing copy placed over it.
- Decorative parallax, repeated pins, custom cursors, horizontal homepage scrolling, autoplay galleries, kinetic footers, or WebGL spectacle.

## 5. Homepage controlling journey

The homepage is not a plot about one house. It is a controlled movement from **recognition to confidence**:

`Enter the Studio Bespoke world`
→ `Meet the personal practice behind it`
→ `Understand what the studio can take responsibility for`
→ `Recognize the range and quality of the work`
→ `Experience one project in greater depth`
→ `Understand how the studio works and why it can be trusted`
→ `Reach a calm, informed invitation`

### Target emotional progression

1. Calm authority.
2. Curiosity.
3. Taste recognition.
4. Capability belief.
5. Trust.
6. Readiness to inquire.

## 7. Section contracts

### 7.1 Brand Threshold / Hero

**Job:** Establish category, taste, and confidence in the first three seconds.

**Must communicate:** Studio Bespoke is an interior design practice with a distinctive body of work. The hero is about the brand's world, not one project's renovation problem.

**Working copy boundary:** The existing “Interior Design” and “Not just a pretty space” territory may be preserved for early prototypes because it is current public brand language. Any replacement headline requires a separate copy decision and must not invent a new promise.

**Static-first requirement:** The hero must work with one still image, legible studio identity, stable navigation, a meaningful heading, and a clear path into the page.

**Candidate concept tests:**

1. **Curated room film:** several verified Studio Bespoke spaces edited into one restrained brand sequence.
2. **Living still:** one exceptional room with minimal light/crop movement; lowest asset and performance risk.
3. **Spatial threshold:** a project detail or architectural opening reveals a wider room without turning the hero into a Mira case study.

**Reject:** rapid showreel editing, unrelated AI rooms, long preloader theatre, looping video that never yields to the page, or copy competing with the focal room.

**Asset gate:** A brand-wide hero film cannot be approved until image/video rights, project consistency, poster frame, mobile crop, text-safe zone, and failed-media fallback are known.

### 7.2 The Studio / Founder

**Job:** Make “personal” credible through authorship rather than a long biography.

**Content priority:**

1. What kind of studio this is.
2. How the founder's involvement affects the work.
3. Relevant education/experience, attributed to the studio.
4. Link to the full About page.

**Static-first requirement:** A portrait and concise copy must form one deliberate editorial composition. If the portrait is unavailable or unsuitable, the fallback is a text-led studio statement paired with verified project/process imagery.

**Motion posture:** Calm. Motion may connect the founder to a drawing, material, or finished space, but biography does not need spectacle.

### 7.3 Expertise / Services

**Job:** Convert broad service labels into understandable responsibility.

**Verified service families:** Full interior design and international interior design. Exact labels and stage counts must follow the current approved source at implementation time.

**Static-first requirement:** The visitor can understand the distinction, scope, and next step without opening an accordion or triggering animation.

**Candidate treatments:**

- A composed two-path editorial spread.
- One spatial field where service annotations form around a plan, room, or material set.
- A sequential service explanation using ordinary vertical flow.

**Reject:** generic icon cards, six equal process boxes, jargon-heavy lists, or motion that makes the service harder to compare.

### 7.4 Selected Work / Range

**Job:** Establish breadth before asking one project to carry detailed proof.

**Selection principle:** Fewer, stronger projects. Lead with residential work if it reflects the observed current positioning, then use a carefully chosen contrasting project only when it clarifies range.

**Project eligibility:** A project may enter the homepage only when its name, category, image family, and destination are verified. Captions may not invent client goals, constraints, budgets, locations, materials, or outcomes.

**Static-first requirement:** An asymmetric, art-directed selection with one dominant project and subordinate entries. It must not collapse into equal cards.

**Candidate enhancements:**

- Slow gallery procession.
- Crop-to-crop focal transfer.
- Controlled layout formation.
- User-controlled image preview.

Native horizontal homepage scrolling and autoplay galleries remain prohibited.

### 7.5 Featured Project / Mira

**Job:** Demonstrate depth and design judgment after the visitor already understands the brand and range.

**Recommended reuse of current prototype:**

1. Enter through the monochrome architectural drawing/video.
2. Crossfade into the corresponding coloured completed room.
3. Release into two or three additional verified Mira views.
4. Pair each view with concise factual annotation only when supported.
5. End with a direct path to the complete Mira project.

**Motion job:** Transform drawing or planning intent into inhabitable space. The transition is meaningful here because it operates as project proof rather than generic hero atmosphere.

**Static-first and fallback:** Drawing/poster followed by completed room and a normal vertical image sequence. Reduced motion uses an immediate or user-triggered crossfade. Mobile uses short ordinary scenes without a long pin.

**Evidence gate:** Do not call the drawing a “before plan,” “original plan,” or specific intervention document unless provenance confirms that claim.

### 7.6 Approach / Trust

**Job:** Explain responsibility after desire and visual proof have been earned.

**Content candidates:** Verified stages, concept-to-completion coordination, founder authority, collaboration model, and one permitted client observation.

**Static-first requirement:** A clear process that remains useful without imagery or animation.

**Motion posture:** A living-proof system may reveal stages or material decisions quietly. No repeated cinematic pin and no bureaucratic process theatre.

### 7.7 Recognition / Client Proof

**Job:** Confirm—not manufacture—authority.

**Hierarchy:** Project proof first, publication/testimonial confirmation second.

**Static-first requirement:** One publication reference and a limited number of short, legible excerpts. Full testimonials remain available elsewhere if needed.

**Reject:** logo walls, carousels that autoplay long quotations, star-rating decoration, or publication branding louder than Studio Bespoke.

### 7.8 Inquiry / Footer

**Job:** Convert readiness into a low-friction next step.

**Static-first requirement:** Contact route, focus states, labels, error messaging, privacy/legal access, and keyboard operation work without motion.

**Motion posture:** Interface feedback only.

**Activation gate:** No functional form, `mailto`, phone number, address, or social link may be enabled until its destination is confirmed.

## 9. Concept exploration required before choreography

The blueprint does not yet select a visual territory. Three structurally different directions must be tested against the same section contracts.

### Territory A — The Living Portfolio

Media-led and project-rich. A restrained cinematic opening establishes the brand, selected work creates the main rhythm, and Mira becomes the deepest chapter.

**Strength:** Closest to Studio Bespoke's existing photo-first identity while allowing a meaningful cinematic upgrade.
**Risk:** Requires disciplined curation and consistent high-quality media.

### Territory B — The Bespoke Practice

Founder- and expertise-led editorial direction. The personal practice and complete-service responsibility arrive earlier; projects prove the claims.

**Strength:** Strong trust and commercial clarity with moderate asset burden.
**Risk:** Could become too explanatory or conservative if project imagery loses authority.

### Territory C — Spatial Thresholds

The most choreographed direction. Architectural openings, crop transfers, drawing-to-room transformation, and composed scene changes create a spatial journey.

**Strength:** Highest distinctiveness and cinematic potential.
**Risk:** Highest motion, asset, mobile, performance, and maintenance burden; easiest to overdesign.

No hybrid is approved. A hybrid may be considered only after the three territories are visibly tested and its governing logic is stated.

## 10. Motion selection protocol

Motion may be proposed only after answering:

1. What should the visitor believe at this moment?
2. What verified content carries that belief?
3. What does movement reveal that a still composition cannot?
4. What is the mobile equivalent?
5. What is the reduced-motion equivalent?
6. What happens when the media or JavaScript fails?
7. What does the effect cost in performance and maintenance?

### Candidate motion families by job

| Communication job | Candidate family | Stillness alternative |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Establish atmosphere | Curated room film or living still | One commanding project image |
| Reveal a room | Crop/threshold reveal or layered depth | Intentional wide-room composition |
| Show transformation | Plan/video-to-room crossfade | Paired drawing and final image |
| Demonstrate range | Gallery procession or controlled layout formation | Asymmetric editorial project sequence |
| Explain service responsibility | Spatial annotations or quiet stage progression | Two-path service spread |
| Slow into inquiry | Deliberate visual release and reduced density | Still, quiet inquiry field |

The motion library is consulted by communication job only. No effect may rewrite the section order, proof hierarchy, brand content, or accessibility contract.

## 11. Responsive, accessibility, and resilience contract

### Desktop

- One dominant visual or typographic read per viewport.
- No more than one substantial pinned/scroll-scrubbed sequence unless later evidence proves a second is necessary.
- Navigation and inquiry remain reachable during all authored sequences.

### Mobile

- Mobile is composed independently, not generated by stacking desktop layers.
- No required hover, native horizontal homepage scroll, long cinematic pin, or tiny over-image annotations.
- Project crops preserve depth, room identity, and the content being discussed.
- Video is optional; poster and still sequences are first-class states.

### Reduced motion

- All information and project access remain available.
- Scrubbed movement becomes static sequence, direct state change, or user-controlled reveal.
- Ambient media does not autoplay.
- Typography, hierarchy, and pacing retain authority without animation.

### Accessibility

- One meaningful `h1`, ordered headings, semantic sections and landmarks.
- Skip link, visible focus, keyboard-operable navigation and interactions.
- Accurate project-specific alternative text; decorative media hidden from assistive technology.
- Text is never dependent on an unsafe moving-image zone for readability.
- Forms, when activated, include labels, instructions, errors, success, and no motion-dependent feedback.

### Performance and failure

- Only one first-viewport media system.
- Responsive images, intrinsic dimensions, poster frames, and below-fold lazy loading.
- Offscreen video pauses; no multiple autoplay room films.
- Failed video resolves to a deliberate poster without hiding copy or navigation.
- Failed JavaScript leaves a complete semantic homepage.

## 13. Iteration and approval system

Each section moves through these statuses:

`Evidence ready` → `Static concept options` → `Direction approved` → `Static prototype` → `Motion candidate` → `Motion approved` → `Enhanced prototype` → `Verified`

Rules:

1. Review one meaningful slice at a time.
2. Never build a motion treatment before its static composition communicates correctly.
3. Present alternatives with reasons, asset cost, performance cost, and failure risk.
4. User feedback may revise a section, but changes that alter the page's controlling journey return to the blueprint gate.
5. A reference contributes a principle, not automatically its layout, copy, intensity, or effect.
6. Preserve unrelated user changes and do not commit, push, deploy, publish, or submit forms without explicit instruction.
