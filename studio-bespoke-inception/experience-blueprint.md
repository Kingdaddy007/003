# Studio Bespoke Design — Experience Blueprint

**Status:** Selected-concept architecture  
**Selected territory:** The Ritual Plan  
**Controlling argument:** A home becomes personal when its plan begins to recognise the life inside it.

## 1. Brand-to-scene translation

| Strategic input | Experience translation |
| --- | --- |
| Perception gap | Replace “tasteful warm-minimal decorator” with a visible renovation intelligence story. |
| Desired belief | Studio Bespoke can take responsibility for how a home functions, feels and endures. |
| Taste world | Warm architectural domesticity: framed light, calm material planes, timber, stone/plaster, concealed utility, lived-in restraint. |

## 3. Information hierarchy and navigation

- **Primary navigation:** Work, Approach, Studio, Journal/Press, Enquire. It must remain reachable during pinned chapters.
- **First hierarchy:** The Mira argument, not the logo, service list, founder portrait or a CTA.
- **Proof hierarchy:** Mira → independent editorial validation → exact decisions → selected supporting work → process/fit → inquiry.
- **Portfolio rule:** A project first appears as a decision narrative. A wider project index follows only after the studio’s judgment is proven.
- **Inquiry rule:** There is no high-pressure opening CTA. An understated persistent “Discuss your home” link may be available but the primary invitation arrives only after proof.

## 4. Visual system direction

### Composition

- One dominant read per viewport. Avoid card-grid rhythms.
- Use wide, deep room photographs to establish architecture; use close material crops only after the visitor knows what they explain.
- Keep copy in known text-safe areas: quiet wall plane, floor margin, or a dedicated annotation field—not over furniture or a face.
- Let captions behave like architectural notes: small, exact, anchored to image edges.
- Use silence after dense proof; empty space must frame, pause, or prepare a decision.

### Typography

- Display type must operate as a spatial mass: restrained but decisive, never fragile or over-tracked.
- Body type is a quiet editorial narrator.
- Annotations carry project facts, material moves and constraints.
- Typography may not imitate a fashion magazine without a grid and proof purpose.

### Functional colour plan

Colour is **image-led and supporting**, not the primary identity carrier. Exact tokens wait until source images are sampled from licensed assets.

| Role | Direction | Job |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Neutral | mineral paper / warm off-white | reading field and visual rest |
| Communicator | near-charcoal | high-legibility display, proof, navigation |
| Anchor | muted olive-grey or mineral brown sampled from Mira | captions, rules, quiet continuity |
| Support | soft plaster / stone tone | register transition and containers |
| Action | one deep, accessible mineral accent | enquiry and key interactive focus only |

Every final token must pass contrast, grayscale, mobile, and colour-vision checks before implementation.

## 5. Motion concept

### Physical law

**A settled residence moves slowly, with weight.** Architecture does not bounce; light travels gradually; an arch reveals the next room; a material edge creates a transition. Acceleration is low and motion leaves the user in control.

### Approved tracks

| Track | Role | Approved behaviour | Excluded behaviour |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Arrival | Open the first threshold | One-time light/curtain-like reveal into Mira; headline remains stable | Long loading theatre, logo animation, autoplay film |
| Ambient | Give a room subtle life | Optional low-amplitude light/shadow movement only when a real asset supports it | Constant floating objects, particles, decorative parallax |
| Scroll-bound | Bind argument to proof | One pinned Mira proof sequence driven by a short room film/frame sequence; slow gallery procession for selected decisions | Pinning every section, scroll-jacking, repeated fade-ups |
| Interaction | Give agency where it improves understanding | Tap/click an annotation to reveal a decision; clear form focus states | Hover-only critical content, novelty cursor effects |

### GSAP decision

**GSAP + ScrollTrigger is approved** for the arrival sequence, one scrubbed/pinned flagship proof chapter, and a measured gallery procession. The flagship proof may use a short, reverse-safe Mira room film rendered as a canvas frame sequence, so scroll position precisely controls its playhead. It is not approved for every transition. Lenis is not assumed; native scroll remains the default until a prototype demonstrates that a smooth-scroll layer is needed and remains accessible.

## 6. Responsive intent

- Desktop may use an authored Mira hold/pin because large imagery can retain spatial depth.
- Mobile preserves the same chapter order but converts pins into ordinary, short sequential scenes with clear static states.
- No mobile visitor is required to scrub, hover, or wait through an animation to access proof or inquiry.
- Crops must retain the kitchen-to-living relationship or be replaced with an image that does.
- Reduced-motion is a fully designed editorial sequence, not a broken animation-free fallback.

## 7. Inquiry architecture

**Invitation line:** “Tell us what no longer works in the way you live at home.”

The inquiry should establish project fit before collecting details: home type/location, scope, desired start period, the daily ritual or spatial frustration to resolve, and how the visitor found the studio. It should state the selective capacity/lead-time posture honestly and avoid claiming outcomes not operationally verified.

## 8. Architecture risks and verification

| Risk | Prevention | Test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ritual language becomes vague | Every ritual receives a named architectural decision | Content review against Mira and supporting projects |
| Cinematic motion hides weak proof | Motion appears only after a communication job is documented | Remove motion and confirm the static sequence still argues clearly |
| Project image family is repetitive | Deliberate mix of wide, threshold, material and use cues | Desktop and mobile crop review |
| Inquiry arrives too late or too vaguely | Keep a reachable nav inquiry and end with explicit fit prompt | Task test with a prospective homeowner scenario |
| Mobile loses staged quality | Design mobile states independently | Prototype at narrow viewport before full build |
