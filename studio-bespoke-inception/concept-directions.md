## Territory A — The Ritual Plan

### Premise

**A home becomes personal when its plan begins to recognise the life inside it.**

### Whole-page logic

1. Opening plan — wide Mira image and a statement about a home learning its inhabitants.
2. Intervention — walls, circulation and kitchen as first proof.
3. Rituals made spatial — coffee, hosting, storage, retreat and guest care as design decisions.
4. Material as consequence — joinery, stone, microcement and arches shown as function plus atmosphere.
5. Other lives, other answers — Zulal and Desert Leaf demonstrate scale.
6. Managed journey — three stages and selected capacity appear after trust.
7. Inquiry threshold — describe the part of life at home that currently does not work.

### Posture, assets and risk

Architectural and quietly immersive; still imagery primary. Optional motion could move attention from a plan/problem caption to its result. Needs wide Mira imagery, any available plan/site/sketch material, named decisions, and a small supporting project set.

**Riskiest assumption:** Visitors find ritual-led spatial logic as compelling as a conventional luxury-room reveal.  
**Failure condition:** Without plans, process images or exact decisions, the premise becomes poetry over photography and should simplify into a project-led editorial.

## Territory B — The House Relearns You

### Premise

**A renovation is a sequence of corrections: dark becomes open, cluttered becomes calm, accidental becomes intentional.**

### Whole-page logic

1. Name an unworking domestic condition.
2. Make Mira’s wall removal and kitchen-centred plan the decisive cut.
3. Follow its effects on light, flow, storage and connection.
4. Use Desert Leaf and Zulal as micro-transformations.
5. Make delivery responsibility credible through materials, joinery and project management.
6. State capacity and fit.

### Posture, assets and risk

Contrast-led and causal. Motion is conditional and only useful for real old-state/new-state transfer.

**Riskiest assumption:** Authentic before/process proof exists.  
**Failure condition:** If it does not, generic sliders or invented diagrams would erode trust. This direction must not advance without genuine transformation evidence.

## Territory C — A Quiet Case for Change

### Premise

**The most credible luxury renovation is a quiet accumulation of decisions that become obvious only when you live with them.**

### Whole-page logic

1. One still composed opening.
2. Marie Claire, one named fact and one client line establish authority.
3. Three Mira decisions become precise captioned evidence.
4. A small project collection shows range.
5. Founder education, delivery responsibility and partner network appear as annotations, not biography.
6. A single calm inquiry question concludes.

### Posture, assets and risk

The still-led option. It behaves like an exhibition catalogue: wide images, exact captions, deep silence and almost no UI. Motion remains `none` unless a prototype proves a real attention job.

**Riskiest assumption:** Proof-first restraint can create enough desire.  
**Failure condition:** If image scale/depth/detail is too repetitive, restraint becomes empty instead of controlled.

## Neutral comparison and director verdict

- **A** best fits the public evidence. It is emotionally specific and spatially grounded without needing fabricated before/after material. It still needs precise captions and modest process evidence.
- **B** is most commercially direct, but it cannot advance until authentic before/process assets are secured.
- **C** is most achievable with existing public assets and has lowest performance risk; it needs rigorous typography/crop discipline to avoid becoming a quiet template.

**Director verdict:** Pass. These are three whole-page arguments, not palette or hero variations. No territory is selected here.

## Selection record — 2026-07-14

**User selection:** Territory A — **The Ritual Plan**.

### Decision rationale

The selected direction best repairs the perception gap while remaining truthful to the available evidence. Mira gives the site a concrete, independently documented example of a home reorganised around daily life. The direction creates a cinematic, project-led story without fabricating before/after proof or forcing the restrained work into a generic portfolio layout.

### Rejected alternatives

- **Territory B — The House Relearns You:** rejected for now because authentic paired before/process evidence has not been verified. Its transformation promise would otherwise be stronger than the available proof.
- **Territory C — A Quiet Case for Change:** retained as a fallback posture for mobile, reduced-motion, or asset-limited execution. It is credible but less emotionally specific than the chosen direction.

### Motion decision

Motion is approved as a storytelling instrument, not a decorative layer. The selected direction permits a short arrival threshold, one or two pinned scroll-bound proof moments, and scene-native transitions based on light, architectural openings, and material surfaces. It rejects generic fade-up stacks, autoplay video wallpaper, and scroll effects without a proof job.

### Return conditions

Return to concept exploration if the studio cannot supply enough project-specific Mira evidence to support the ritual/plan narrative, or if a vertical prototype shows that mobile and reduced-motion versions lose clarity. In that case, re-evaluate Territory C before adding spectacle.
