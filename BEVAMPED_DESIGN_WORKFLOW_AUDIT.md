# BEVAMPED Design Workflow Audit

**Date:** 2026-07-13  
**Status:** Workflow refactor implemented; Project 003 website not started

## Audit Question

How should BEVAMPED discover, select, test, and execute distinctive cinematic website concepts for interior-design prospects without repeating a house template or collecting effects opportunistically?

## Central Diagnosis

The existing OS was strong at specifying and criticizing a concept after one existed, but weak at discovering, comparing, and validating the concept itself.

It prematurely required a fixed narrative, physical hero event, anchor object, spatial metaphor, audit mechanics, motion-library effect, and fourteen project artifacts. Visual brainstorming occurred after these decisions. This converted useful heuristics into a template and moved creative risk into late-stage improvisation.

The user's bottleneck was not lack of technical ambition. The two prior builds show an ability to execute complex cinematic ideas. The bottleneck was a missing selection system: deciding which idea belongs to the brand, which references answer a real problem, which motion is necessary, and whether the concept can govern the entire page.

## Keep / Adapt / Remove / Add

### Keep

- evidence-based brand diagnosis and perception-gap thinking
- proof architecture and selective inquiry
- asset, performance, accessibility, and fallback discipline
- separation of motion responsibilities
- Master Design Director critique and subtraction
- rich technical motion references
- niche specialization in interior and spatial brands

### Adapt

- project inception: from artifact production to evidence, divergence, selection, and validation
- brand strategy: diagnose the problem without prescribing the visual answer
- storytelling: use multiple candidate forms rather than one universal sequence
- visual brainstorm: move before concept commitment
- spatial concept: synthesize an approved territory rather than invent one invisibly
- cinematic motion: select by semantic job after concept selection
- scroll storyboard: conditional for genuine scroll complexity
- motion library: route by communication purpose, asset fit, risk, and fallback
- director critique: add gates at brief, territories, blueprint, vertical slice, and final experience

### Remove

- mandatory `Atmosphere -> Taste -> Transformation -> Proof -> Method -> Inquiry`
- mandatory physical hero event, anchor object, room metaphor, or video opener
- mandatory design-audit citations and motion-library effect
- fourteen-file pre-build gate
- the overlapping `cinematic-showroom-strategy` skill
- doctrine-level requirements for GSAP, Lenis, continuous scroll motion, gold, or other house-style mechanics

### Add

- sourced evidence dossier with fact/inference separation
- decision-ready creative brief
- three concept territories with purposeful references and rough tests
- comparison scorecard with judgment and risk assumptions
- consolidated experience blueprint and production plan
- semantic motion index
- highest-risk prototype before broad production
- representative vertical slice before full-page expansion
- preserved rejected directions and decision rationale

## Revised Workflow

`Evidence -> Brand diagnosis -> Creative brief -> Concept territories -> Purpose-led references + rough styleframes/prototypes -> Concept selection -> Experience blueprint -> Production plan -> Risk prototype -> Vertical slice -> Full production -> Critique + verification`

## Professional Iteration Principle

Professionals do not avoid iteration. They reduce random iteration by externalizing concepts early, choosing against criteria, and testing the riskiest assumption. Later changes are smaller when the premise survives; major revision remains legitimate when evidence disproves it.
