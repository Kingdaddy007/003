# Creative Brief: Studio Bespoke Design

**Status:** Active Brief Contract (Independently Authored)  
**Research Date:** 2026-07-14  
**Target:** Studio Bespoke Design (Dubai, UAE)  
**Founder:** Brittany Guimaraes  

---

## 1. Core Strategic Problem

### Current State
Studio Bespoke has the capacity to execute elite residential renovations (e.g., Al Barari) and conscious commercial spaces (New Earth Café) that are structurally complex and editorially recognized (*Marie Claire Maison*). However, the current website presents them as a generic, template-driven decoration practice. The site has broken widgets (stuck at `0`), duplicate portfolio cards, legacy text ("Full turkey packages"), and critical spelling discrepancies in their contact emails and social media handles. 

### Desired State
A high-net-worth homeowner in Dubai should immediately recognize the studio as a high-integrity, sustainable interior architecture practice. The site must prove they can handle major structural changes and custom joinery detailing, shifting focus from surface decoration to thoughtful spatial re-planning.

---

## 2. Brand Positioning: "The Architecture of Everyday Rituals"

### The Positioning Wedge
Move the studio away from generic "quiet luxury" styling and establish them as **architects of daily life**. Their defensible edge is translating how a family lives (e.g., baking, morning coffee rituals, wellness) into structural layout decisions, custom carpentry, and sustainable materials (LEED GA credentials).

### The Enemy (To Reject)
- **Cliche Luxury:** Shiny marble floors, chrome rails, gold trims, heavy serif headers, and generic stock villa video loops.
- **Contractor Brochure:** Generic icons for services (hammers, saws), loud CTAs ("Get a Free Quote"), and unannotated grids of finished rooms.

### The Counter-Standard
*The traditional decorator says: "A home is defined by beautiful furniture."*  
*Studio Bespoke says: "A home begins with changing how you live: where the light lands, how you move, where you store what you own, and how your daily rituals are supported."*

---

## 3. Communication Jobs & Visual System Directives

### Key Communication Jobs
1. **Architectural Pedigree:** Highlight Brittany's LEED GA status, international firm background, and Gensler Design Director endorsement.
2. **Flagship Transformation:** Ground the site around the Mira kitchen transformation—showing how structural wall-removal and custom joinery solved a client's baking and hosting patterns.
3. **Material Detailing:** Showcase custom millwork, raw terracotta, and clay joints to prove technical execution.
4. **Trust Restoration:** Clean up the duplicate portfolio entries and resolve the contact channel email typos.

### Visual & Sensation Directives
- **Palette:** Earth tones, raw terracotta, warm clay plaster, and natural oiled oak.
- **Typography:** Spacious, modern serifs paired with clean, wide-spaced sans-serif details.
- **Motion Sensation:** Still, calm, and slow. Wipes or fades should mimic the walking pace of entering a house or opening an oak sliding screen.
- **Inquiry Tone:** Restricted, selective concierge invitation. It sets expectations about project timelines and scale.
