## 2. Design Questions & Reference Provenance

- **Question:** How do we prove spatial transformation without before-state photos?
  - *Provenance:* The *Marie Claire Maison* article details the physical floor plan adjustments (wall removal, powder room relocation).
  - *Lesson:* Focus the narrative on the architectural layout shifts and custom carpentry annotations, describing the inherited problem and the designed solution.

  - *Provenance:* Sourcing clay and terracotta tiles for the New Earth Café conscious eatery.

## 3. Territory A — The Ritual Plan

### Premise
**A home becomes personal when its floor plan begins to recognize the life inside it.**

### Visitor Journey (Whole-Page Sequence)
1. **The Threshold (Atmosphere):** A large, warm crop of the Mira kitchen under dappled morning light, accompanied by a quiet thesis statement: *A home should learn how you live.*
2. **The Flagship Proof (Mira):** Visual and narrative breakdown of the kitchen renovation, mapping structural wall-removals to the client's baking and hosting rituals.
3. **The Ritual Sequence:** Subsections focusing on other daily routines (e.g., the morning coffee ritual showing the concealed nook, wellness-oriented sourcing for New Earth Café).
4. **The Details (Curation):** Close-ups of carpentry joints, microcement textures, and custom tile junctions to showcase detailing.
5. **The Handover (Method):** Brittany's background, LEED certification, and turnkey contractor management process.
6. **The Concierge (Inquiry):** A restricted inquiry form asking the client to describe their home's spatial challenges.

### Visual & Motion Posture
Calm, architectural, and still. If any motion is introduced later, it is scroll-bound and slow, guiding focus from textual layout descriptions to the corresponding spatial zoom.

### Strategic Fit & Risks
- **Why it belongs:** Directly ties the portfolio proof to their strongest differentiator (designing spaces around client habits).
- **Riskiest Assumption:** That visitors will read short narrative descriptions rather than just browsing finished room pictures.

## 4. Territory B — The House Relearns You

### Premise
**A renovation is a sequence of corrections: dark becomes open, compartmentalized becomes connected.**

### Visitor Journey (Whole-Page Sequence)
1. **The Tension (Atmosphere):** An opening line calling out the frustrations of living in an unworking home: *Dark hallways, closed rooms, borrowed habits.*
2. **The Cut (Transformation):** An interactive layout comparison showing the before/after floor plan shift of the Mira ground floor.
3. **The Consequence:** Showing how the structural modifications improved light penetration, family connection, and storage efficiency.
4. **Scale Variation:** Showing that the same discipline applies to smaller spaces (converting a maid's room to a self-contained studio in Desert Leaf).
5. **The Process:** A structured walk-through of the turnkey execution, showing how drawings become finished joinery.
6. **The Gate (Inquiry):** Invitation to schedule an architectural evaluation for homeowners looking to renovate.

### Visual & Motion Posture
Higher contrast. Focuses on before/after transitions, structural diagrams overlaying photographs, and layout animations.

### Strategic Fit & Risks
- **Why it belongs:** Directly speaks to the client's renovation anxieties, establishing the studio as problem-solvers.
- **Riskiest Assumption:** Requires authentic "before" layout sketches or diagrams. If these cannot be obtained, the concept fails.

## 5. Territory C — A Quiet Case for Change

### Premise
**The most credible design is not a spectacle. It is a quiet accumulation of decisions that become obvious only when you live with them.**

### Visitor Journey (Whole-Page Sequence)
1. **The Silence (Atmosphere):** A single, highly composed architectural photograph of the Lakes villa. No headers, only a minimal subtitle and logo.
2. **The Editorial Proof:** Displays the *Marie Claire Maison* and *Gensler UAE* endorsements in a compact, high-density editorial section.
3. **The Case File:** A small, curated index of projects (Mira, New Earth Café, Lakes), showing only one high-quality shot and one layout annotation per project.
4. **The Materials:** High-fidelity crops of raw timber, terracotta, plaster, and brass, detailing their functional selection.
5. **The Studio Posture:** Stating their yearly client capacity and selective design-driven process.
6. **The Invitation:** A quiet contact link opening a structured, minimal email template.

### Visual & Motion Posture
Minimal, still-led, resembling a physical art exhibition or architecture monograph. No video backgrounds, no layout sliders.

### Strategic Fit & Risks
- **Why it belongs:** Positions the studio as an elite, selective brand, using restraint to establish authority.
- **Riskiest Assumption:** Requires extremely high-fidelity photography and impeccable typography spacing. If the images feel generic, the page will look empty.

## 6. Structural Divergence Check

| Dimension | Territory A (Ritual Plan) | Territory B (House Relearns You) | Territory C (Quiet Case) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Governing Idea** | Spaces must fit daily habits. | Renovation corrects structural errors. | True quality is felt, not shouted. |
| **First Impression** | Warm, personal home focus. | High-contrast problem statement. | Minimalist editorial silence. |
| **First Proof** | Mira baking-ritual kitchen. | Mira floor plan structural comparison. | Editorial press feature + quotes. |
| **Page Rhythm** | Habit-to-space narrative chapters. | Problem-to-solution sequence. | Exhibition catalog / case file. |
| **Asset Needs** | Good completed photos + sketches. | High (needs plans and before photos). | Moderate (high-res finished photos). |
| **Motion Vibe** | Slow scroll-bound layout shifts. | Before/after comparison slides. | Zero motion (strictly static). |

---

## 7. Strategic Recommendation

- **Recommended:** **Territory A (The Ritual Plan)**. It aligns perfectly with the available client testimonials (which are highly personal and relationship-driven) and leverages the Marie Claire feature details without requiring missing before-state photography (the main risk of Territory B). It feels warm, architectural, and premium.
- **Alternative:** **Territory C (A Quiet Case for Change)** is the safest implementation route if asset collection proves difficult, relying entirely on their high-quality finished project imagery and editorial status.
