## 2. Facts and Evidence-Backed Observations

### Business, Credentials & Founder Authority
- **Fact:** The studio is based in Dubai, UAE, and operates in the high-end residential interior architecture and renovation market.
- **Fact:** The founder is Brittany Guimaraes. She holds BA and Honours degrees in Interior Design.
- **Reported:** Brittany has over 10 years of experience in Dubai, having worked at both large international design firms and small boutique practices before launching her studio.
- **Reported:** Stated to hold LEED GA (Green Associate) certification, indicating a background in sustainable design methodologies.
- **Fact:** The studio's work is recognized in print and online media. A 2026 feature in *Marie Claire Maison* covers their "Mira" renovation under the title *"The Architecture of Everyday Rituals"*.
- **Fact:** Stated design focus is coffee-adjacent (bio mentions a *"love or some might say obsession with coffee. Life of a designer right."*).

### Demonstrated Design and Project Capabilities
- **Fact (Mira Renovation):** The project involved heavy structural alterations. They removed multiple partition walls on the ground floor to open the layout, relocated a powder room, introduced arched partitions, and built custom cabinetry with integrated storage and a concealed coffee nook.
- **Fact (Zulal - The Lakes):** Designed a joinery and material update around the constraint of existing grey slate flooring, utilizing microcement and brushed brass.
- **Fact (Desert Leaf - Al Barari):** Transformed a small maid's room into a self-contained, light-filled guest studio using custom millwork.
- **Fact (New Earth Café - Al Barari):** Designed a conscious commercial café using custom-sourced terracotta and clay tiles, earthy plaster finishes, and colors mapped to chakra zones.
- **Fact (Diane Thorsen Endorsement):** Diane Thorsen (Design Director, Gensler UAE) confirms Brittany's technical skills, refined design sense, and capability to translate concepts into functional layouts.

### Forensic Website Gaps & Premium Leaks
- **Identity/Email Typo Conflict:** On the contact page body, the contact email is hardcoded as `info@studiobspoke.design` (no "e") and the Instagram link points to `@studiobspoke.design` (no "e"). However, the domain is `studiobespoke.design` (with "e"), and the footer links show `info@studiobespoke.design` (with "e").
- **WordPress Media Naming:** The uploaded logo and site icon assets are named `cropped-Studio-Bspoke_Icon...` and `Studio-Bspoke_Logo...`, showing that the spelling typo runs through the file structure.
- **Broken Counter Animations:** The homepage metrics widgets (Cups of coffee, Years experience, Career projects) render as `0` or fail to animate.
- **Dead Navigation Routes:** The parent "Publications" main menu navigation is a dead hash link (`#`), though its child elements lead to valid external features.
- **Fragmented Project Organization:** The portfolio has duplicates (e.g., `Mira` and `Mira 2` appear as separate projects, and `Zulal - The Lakes` and `Zulal - The Lakes 2` are split), causing the portfolio to read like a disjointed gallery of individual rooms instead of unified client narratives.

## 3. Inferences & Strategic Assumptions

### Budgets and Scale
- **Inference:** Performing heavy structural residential renovations in Al Barari (a high-luxury villa community in Dubai) and designing commercial eateries suggests average project fee levels of $50,000 to $200,000+.
- **Inference:** Homeowners in these communities value sustainable, detailed design over surface-level styling.

### Strategic Outreach Handling
- **Inference:** Because of the email misspelling mismatch on the contact page, potential inquiries sent to the `studiobspoke` address could be lost. Initial outbound contact must prioritize the verified `info@studiobespoke.design` address, phone lines, or Instagram DM.

### Materiality and Style
- **Inference:** The studio's aesthetic values textured plaster, raw timber, natural terracotta, linen, and microcement. It rejects standard glossy finishes, chrome, and over-decorated builder styling.
